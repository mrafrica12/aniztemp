Our CEO

Meet the visionary leader who built Aniz, Inc. from a personal calling into a cornerstone of Atlanta's public health community.

Zina M. Age, LMSW, MAC

President / Founder / CEO

Zina Age is a native of New Orleans, Louisiana and a graduate of Louisiana State University. She holds advanced degrees in Social Work from Clark Atlanta University and Addiction Counseling from the National Association of Forensic Counselors.

Zina began social services work in 1987, focusing on African-American communities affected by HIV/AIDS and health disparities. In 1991, a personal turning point came when her godson was diagnosed with HIV. Unable to find comprehensive support services for children affected by the disease in Metro Atlanta, she resolved to build them herself.

As a graduate intern at Outreach, Inc., she created "We Want To Know" — a structured therapeutic program for children ages 17 and under — which became the seed for Aniz, Inc., incorporated in 1996.

Leadership & Impact

Under Zina's leadership, Aniz, Inc. has grown from a single children's program into a full-service agency offering mental health counseling, HIV prevention, Ryan White services, substance use support, and community outreach throughout Atlanta.

She is a noted public speaker who presents at universities, conferences, and international forums on topics including community mental health, HIV/AIDS prevention, intergenerational HIV spread, discrimination in Black LGBTQ+ communities, and addressing stigma.

Her work has been recognized by the White House, the U.S. Department of Homeland Security, Boys & Girls Clubs of America, and the NAACP Southeast Region.
